CPU Sharing and Server Work

Learn about how STREAM utilises your CPU to help out with things.

When you come onto STREAM, your PC opens up a portal, your device is now essentially a STREAM server for the time being. You will have other devices enter your home network to access STREAM. 80% of your CPU usage gets handed to AdrenalineEngine, where it starts the work.

What type of work is happening? Well simply, your device helps find content for other people while you are searching, it is also able to retrieve information for other users from the Cloud. If your device is currently holding another operation, it will relay search results to another node on the network, or a STREAM server.

Where won't it work? You won't be connected if STREAM cannot ensure a secure connection to the network and if your device cannot verify its remote identity using certificates. You need to have the latest security patches for your platform installed. You cannot be on HTTP, and only be allowed on HTTPS. If a screen recording software is detected, or you open up your browsers development tools, then all operations are immediately aborted and you will be disconnected.

Why does it happen? STREAM wants to keep the worlds information efficiently connected together. We are trying to create the Internet Of Things for STREAM. Essentially, things like traffic lights, game consoles, bus information signs are able to relay STREAMs server signals.

Can I remove myself from the network? The only way to remove yourself is to immediately close this page and stop using STREAM entirely. Remember: This is only supported by STREAM, and no other third party hoster with a STREAM Custom Search Engine will have this installed.

If you want an alternative search engine, still using the STREAM Web Index, check out our list of CSEs.
